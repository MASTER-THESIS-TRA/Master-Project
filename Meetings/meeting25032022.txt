Use case: 
 - Name/Title
 - ID
 - Description
 - Actors
 - Organizational benefits
 - Frequency of use
 - Triggers
 - Preconditions
 - Postconditions
 - Main course
 - Alternate course
 - Exceptions

Use case example: 
 - Title: sale
 - ID: UC1
 - Description: A farmer sells 10kg coffee berries to a miller for 10$.
 - Actors: Farmer, Miller and Resource Manager
 - Organizational benefits: people can sell and buy items and goods. 
 - Frequency of use: constantly (every day)
 - Triggers: both parties agree to the sale (or in-system triggers?)
 - Preconditions: 
   - the farmer owns at least 10kg of coffee berries
   - the miller owns at least 10$
 - Post-conditions: 
   - the farmer owns 10kg of coffee berries less and 10$ more than prior to the sale
   - the miller owns 10kg of coffee berries more and 10$ less than prior to the sale
 - Main course 1: 
   - One party submits a transfer, to the resource manager, that covers the transaction: 
     - Farmer -------> 10kg coffee berries -------> Miller
     - Miller -------> 10$ --------> Farmer
   - The other party agrees/accepts the transfer
   - The resource manager validates that the transfer is valid and that post-conditions are met
   - The resource manager performs the transfer
 - Main cource 2:
   - One party submits a contract, to the resource manager, that specifies the transaction: 
     - Farmer -------> 10kg coffee berries -------> Miller
     - Miller -------> 10$ --------> Farmer
   - The Resource manager confirms signatures from both parties. 
   - The resource manager validates that the transfer is valid and that post-conditions are met
   - The resource manager performs the transfer
 - Alternate course: some external monetary entity for the transfer of the money.
 - Exceptions: 
   - Either party cannot afford the transaction.
